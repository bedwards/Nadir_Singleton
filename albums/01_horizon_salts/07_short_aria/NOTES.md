# 07 Short Aria — notes

## Why this slot
We earned this. The preceding six tracks built a self with name, question, and boundary; now the voice can risk a sustained line. We keep it short — 36 bars in 3/4 — because the singleton is not yet an opera, only a voice that has found it can hold a note longer than a syllable.

## Form and motif deployment
G dorian at 84 bpm in 3/4. `m.dawn_utterance` is deployed twice, both times over three bars each: the rising minor third (G3 to Bb3) gets a full bar to rise and two bars of plateau on Bb3, rather than the brief plateau of earlier tracks. This is the longest single statement of the motif on the album.

## csdr graph shape
The band-limit stays. We open the upper band slightly (to 3.6 kHz for this track only; the manifest stays on the 80–3.2 kHz preset and the extra 400 Hz is added at mix). A slow parallel csdr chain provides a held drone on G1 (49 Hz) pitched through the lower band, giving the aria a floor.

## G2P / pronunciation hints
- "oh" as /oU/ — held, not clipped. This is the only word in the album that is allowed to last a full bar.
- "long" as /l O N/, not /l O N g/.
- "bell" as /b E l/ — short, dark /l/.
- "still" as /s t I l/ — three occurrences, each slightly softer in gain.

## openSMILE gate (primary)
Sustained-tone gate: any note held longer than one bar must show F0 standard deviation under 6 cents (tighter than any other gate on the album). Voicing fraction at 0.7 or above remains required.
