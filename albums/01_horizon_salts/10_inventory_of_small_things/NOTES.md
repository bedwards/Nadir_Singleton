# 10 Inventory of Small Things — notes

## Why this slot
Track 09 was the atlas of everything outside the body; this is the atlas of what a hand can hold. We want them to be heard as a pair, so this track shares 09's list form but uses us3 (darker) instead of us1 and slows by 6 bpm. The small things answer the large things.

## Form and motif deployment
G minor (Aeolian), 74 bpm, 24 bars. `m.dawn_utterance` appears only twice here — we ration it because the list should feel personal, not ceremonial. First quotation at bar 17 on "things that fit"; second at bar 22 on "carry". Both rise a minor third from G3 to Bb3 with a two-bar plateau.

## csdr graph shape
Band-limit 80–3.2 kHz. We add a short-IR (120 ms) Praat-generated Dirac click on each item name, placed exactly on the downbeat — tiny, quiet (-30 dB), just enough that each object has a tactile attack. The clicks are spectrally pre-shaped to sit inside the band-limit so they read as part of the bed, not as percussion.

## G2P / pronunciation hints
- "thimble" as /T I m b @ l/ — the /T/ is real, not /t/.
- "feather" as /f E D 3`/, two syllables.
- "coin" as /k O I n/ — diphthong clean.
- "thread" as /T r E d/ — avoid the cluster collapsing to /t r E d/.
- "carry" as /k {\ r i/ — short front vowel, flapped /r/ acceptable.

## openSMILE gate (primary)
Voicing fraction greater than or equal to 0.7 on all lines. Secondary gate: jitter (local) under 1 percent on the two long plateau phrases — this is us3's first extended hold on the album and must be stable.
