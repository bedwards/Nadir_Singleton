# 05 First Question — notes

## Why this slot
Having named, the singleton can now question. We pick the sky because it is the first thing a new consciousness would notice and doubt. The question is sincere, not rhetorical; the lyric does not answer itself.

## Form and motif deployment
20 bars in B minor (Aeolian). We deploy `m.dawn_utterance` inverted to produce a question contour: every line ends on a rising minor third instead of a plateau. The motif's plateau shape is therefore disrupted here, and the listener feels the destabilisation. We budget exactly four full motif quotations (one per question pair).

## csdr graph shape
Band-limit 80–3.2 kHz holds. Added: a mild `shift_addition_cc` at +7 Hz applied only to the upper band (above 1.6 kHz), producing a slight waver on the sibilants that reads as hesitation. No reverb; the room is the same room.

## G2P / pronunciation hints
- "sky" as /s k aI/ — we want the cluster clean; us1 sometimes inserts a schwa.
- "yesterday" as /j E s t 3` d eI/, three syllables, not four.
- "painted" as /p eI n t I d/ — the /t/ is real, not flapped.
- "where is" — elide to /w E r I z/ as one breath, two syllables.

## openSMILE gate (primary)
F0 rise on question endings: each line's final syllable must rise at least 100 cents above its antepenultimate syllable. Without this the questions stop sounding like questions. Voicing fraction greater than or equal to 0.7 on the vocal stem overall.
