# 11 Second Question — notes

## Why this slot
Track 05 asked about the sky (outward). Track 11 asks about the self (inward). Placing them symmetrically — one third of the way in, three quarters of the way in — makes the album hear as a pair of questions around the horizon revealed at track 08.

## Form and motif deployment
E dorian, 76 bpm, 20 bars — identical tempo and bar count to track 05, so the two questions scan as mirrored. `m.dawn_utterance` returns in full here, not inverted as in 05: E3 rising to G3, plateau on G3. The rise reads as a question now only because the lyric phrases it so; the motif shape is unchanged. This is a musical statement that inward questions can be stable in shape even when they are destabilising in content.

## csdr graph shape
Band-limit 80–3.2 kHz. We add a mild `dsb_fc` ring-modulation with a 3 Hz LFO on the mid band (400–1200 Hz) only — a slow pulse that feels like the self checking itself. The outer bands stay clean.

## G2P / pronunciation hints
- "i" as /aI/ — diphthong clear, not collapsed.
- "yesterday" as /j E s t 3` d eI/, matching track 05.
- "wearing" as /w E r I N/, two syllables.
- "morning" as /m O r n I N/, matching tracks 01 and 08.
- "mouth" as /m aU T/, matching track 06.

## openSMILE gate (primary)
Identical to track 05: final-syllable F0 rise of at least 100 cents per question line. Voicing fraction greater than or equal to 0.7. We actively want the two tracks to pass the same gate so the pairing is measurable, not just intended.
