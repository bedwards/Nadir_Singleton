# Old Piano in the Hall — notes

AABA in F major at 72 bpm. 32 bars. Slow, tender, nearly recitative in
places.

## Chord implication
A sections: I-iii-IV-iv | I-vi-ii-V7. The iii-IV-iv swing is the "old-
upright" move. B: VI7-ii-V7-I | IV-iv-I-V7. `m.tin_pan_turnaround` is
abbreviated to a single ii-V-I in the last bar of each A.

## Production shape
- Vox (us3), tessitura 170-460 Hz.
- Ring-mod ghost at 40 Hz is de-tuned by 0.5 Hz to give a slow beating —
  the out-of-tune piano.
- csdr stride drops to a soft once-per-bar pulse. The texture leaves room.
- Klatt texture: a Praat-PSOLA'd vowel sample tuned to F, pitched an octave
  below the vox, sustains under the final A like a plaintive open string.
- We explicitly avoid attacks over the held note so the vocal is the
  foreground.

## Character
Affectionate homage to the instrument at the heart of TPA culture.
`m.hook` is the "It is the honey in the ___" figure, pulling the album's
pervasive "Honey" back into the title frame.
