# The Ragtime Cat — notes

AABA in G major at 128 bpm in 2/4. 32 bars. The most "ragtime" track on
the record — we swing the 16ths on the stride texture.

## Chord implication
A sections: I-VI7-ii-V7 (G-E7-Am-D7) — a secondary dominant gives the
rag its trademark lift. B: IV-II7-V7-V7. `m.tin_pan_turnaround` on final
two bars of each A.

## Production shape
- Vox (us1), tessitura 230-600 Hz — the highest on the album.
- Ring-mod ghost at 40 Hz is tremolo-gated at 4 Hz for a warm wobble.
- csdr stride is swung (delay the off-beat by ~8 ms on alternating eighths).
- Klatt texture: a "meow" formant pair (F1=720, F2=1100) pulsed on the
  three mentions of "cat" — a small joke, not a sound effect.
- Praat Dirac click emulates a tack-piano attack on each stride downbeat.

## Pronunciation hints
- "ragtime" as `'r & g t aI m`.
- "ribbon" as `'r I b @ n`.
- "piano" as `p i '& n oU`.
- "yellow" as `'j E l oU`, "mellow" as `'m E l oU` — rhyme pair, keep the
  double-L soft.
- "turnaround" as `'t @ r n @ r aU n d` — space the `rn` so it doesn't smear.

## Character
We love this cat. The song is affectionate, slightly silly, never smug.
`m.hook` is the two-bar "He loves a little melody / tune" cadence.
