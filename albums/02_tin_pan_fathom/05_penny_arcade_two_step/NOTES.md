# Penny Arcade Two-Step — notes

Strophic in Bb major at 120 bpm in 2/4 — TPA two-step proper. 32 bars = 4
eight-bar verses, each ending with the same two-bar refrain.

## Chord implication
Each verse is I-V-I-V | IV-I-ii-V | I-V-I-V | ii-V-I-I — a pocket two-step.
`m.tin_pan_turnaround` is the closing ii-V-I every eighth bar.

## Production shape
- Vox (us1), tessitura 220-560 Hz — we lean brighter for arcade energy.
- Ring-mod ghost at 40 Hz is swung slightly via amplitude envelope so it
  feels like a distant barrel organ through the arcade door.
- csdr stride hits "oom-pah" squarely on each 2/4 measure: low noise-burst
  (Praat click at 120 Hz) on beat 1, filtered chirp on beat 2.
- Klatt texture supplies a short whistle on each "whistle" lyric (word-painting).

## Pronunciation hints
- "penny" as `'p E n i`.
- "whistle" as `'w I s @ l` — skip the `tl` cluster by routing through schwa.
- "fanfare" as `'f & n f E r`; in us1 the rhotic is mild, let it round.
- "silly" as `'s I l i`, double-light.
- "afternoon" as `& f t @ 'n u n` — two syllables with a clear schwa bridge.

## Character
We are cheerful without being goofy. The two-step is a dance floor invitation.
`m.hook` lands on "dolly on a wire" — the refrain figure that we want to stick.
