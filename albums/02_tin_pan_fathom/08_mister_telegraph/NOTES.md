# Mister Telegraph — notes

12-bar form in C major at 104 bpm. 3 x 12 = 36 bars. We use a diatonic
TPA 12-bar rather than the blues 12-bar.

## Chord implication
Each 12-bar chorus: I-I-I-I | IV-IV-I-I | ii-V-I-V7 — classic TPA
turnaround on the last four bars. `m.tin_pan_turnaround` anchors bars 9-12.

## Production shape
- Vox (us3), tessitura 180-520 Hz.
- Ring-mod ghost at 40 Hz is modulated rhythmically to resemble distant
  dot-dot-dash patterns, derived from Silero-VAD onset gaps in the lead
  vocal (feedback from the singer's breaths sets the Morse cadence).
- csdr texture: a Praat-generated single-click (Dirac) at 1200 Hz stands
  in for the key-tap; placed on syllables we want to mark.
- Klatt texture provides a low telegraph-line hum bed at 120 Hz, one octave
  above the 40 Hz ghost.

## Pronunciation hints
- "telegraph" as `'t E l @ g r & f` — keep the `gr` light.
- "tapping" as `'t & p I N`.
- "message" as `'m E s @ dZ`.
- "copper" as `'k A p @`.
- "weather" as `'w E D @` — the `D` is us1/us3's voiced th diphone.
- We avoid "strength" and "twelfths" entirely.

## Character
Mister Telegraph is devoted and a little lonely. Not sad — he loves the job.
The refrain "He lets the little copper sing" is the `m.hook` line.
