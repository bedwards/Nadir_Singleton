# Overture at the Kiosk — notes

We open the album with a walking-tempo AABA in bright C major. Four 8-bar sections
stack into a 32-bar chorus. The A sections imply I-vi-ii-V; the B section lifts
to IV-iv-I-V7 before the final A returns home.

## Chord implication (monophonic vocal + bed)
Even with only a single melodic line, we trace the changes by landing on chord
tones at bar downbeats: bar 1 C, bar 2 A (as vi), bar 3 D (as ii), bar 4 G (V).
`m.tin_pan_turnaround` supplies the ii-V-I cadence at the end of each A.

## Production shape
- Vox (us1) centred around E4 with tessitura 180-520 Hz.
- Bed: ring-modulated ghost at 40 Hz behind held vowels (m.hook carrier).
- Texture: csdr-built ragtime stride pulse on every quarter, gated by Silero-VAD
  onset map of the lead vocal.
- No bridge solo; a short MBROLA "ooh" passage carries B.

## Pronunciation hints
- "nickel" as `n 'I k @ l` — keep the schwa open so MBROLA diphones flow.
- "awnings" as `'O n I N z` — hold the final nasal short to avoid smearing.
- Avoid hard stops on "hat"; let Praat taper the release.

## Why it opens
A handshake. We put every palette piece on the table so later tracks can
reference without re-introducing. The listener should leave whistling.
