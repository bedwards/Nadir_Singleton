# Moon Over the Awning — notes

Slow AABA ballad in Eb major, 76 bpm. 32 bars, the classic "parlour" tempo.

## Chord implication
A sections: I-vi-ii-V7 twice (Eb-Cm-Fm-Bb7), then iii-VI7-ii-V7 on the
turn. B: IV-iv-I-VI7-ii-V7 (Ab-Abm-Eb-C7-Fm-Bb7) for a little heartbreak.
`m.tin_pan_turnaround` on every A close.

## Production shape
- Vox (us3), tessitura 170-460 Hz — soft, almost whispered on verse 1.
- Ring-mod ghost at 40 Hz, barely there — we dial bed_gain down 6 dB.
- csdr stride is dropped entirely on A sections; returns on B as brushed
  pulse (Praat-shaped noise through a narrow FIR).
- Klatt texture does a single soft "window-pane" harmonic at each chorus
  start — one partial around 880 Hz.

## Pronunciation hints
- "awning" as `'O n I N`.
- "evening" as `'i v n I N` — 2 syllables with a soft `vn` glide.
- "neighbours" as `'n eI b @ z` — no final `rz`.
- "radio" as `'r eI d i oU`.
- "wishing" as `'w I S I N`.
- Skip "ordinary"'s full rhotic; sing `'O r d @ n E r i` with short `r`s.

## Character
We are at peace. This is the album's hush. `m.hook` is the four-word
"Moon over the awning" phrase; it returns in track 18 as a callback.
