# Tin Pan Fathom

_Album 2 of 20 in the Nadir_Singleton corpus._

Vaudeville ghosts of Tin Pan Alley push diphone voices through old forms.
Eighteen short pieces: a ragtime shopfront, a soda fountain, a lighthouse,
a last trolley. Simple diatonic changes. ii-V-I turnarounds. Verse/chorus,
AABA, twelve-bar, and one waltz. MBROLA us1 and us3 voices, Praat PSOLA
tuning, csdr ragtime stride, ring-mod ghost carrier at 40 Hz.

## Motif palette
TPA harmonic turns on diphones. Every track carries `m.tin_pan_turnaround`
and `m.hook` from `albums/CORPUS.md`.

## Production notes

- Every track binds the palette motif `m.tin_pan_turnaround` (ii-V-I on
  scale degrees, ring-mod ghost carrier at 40 Hz) plus `m.hook`.
- Voices alternate us1 (brighter) and us3 (warmer) to keep the album
  surface varied. Tessiturae drift upward in uptempo tracks and downward
  in ballads.
- Characters recur by name: Sally (02), Lil (03), Mister Telegraph (08),
  Bella (17), and a broken metronome, a ragtime cat, a night watchman,
  a piano-playing lady. Track 18 is a roll-call closer that nods to
  every prior track.
- All lyrics are written for MBROLA diphone-safe phonology: we avoid
  clusters like "strength" and "twelfths" and prefer open vowels on
  sustained syllables.
