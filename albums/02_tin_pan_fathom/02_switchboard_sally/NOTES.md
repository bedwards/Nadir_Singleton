# Switchboard Sally — notes

Verse/chorus in F major at 112 bpm. Two 16-bar cycles (verse 8 + chorus 8).

## Chord implication
Verses walk I-vi-IV-V (F-Dm-Bb-C). Choruses loop ii-V-I twice
(`m.tin_pan_turnaround` doubled) then tag on the refrain "Sally, Sally, open
the door". The melody rides the 3rd and 5th through the verse and pivots to
the 6th in the chorus for that classic TPA "lift".

## Production shape
- Vox (us1) carries Sally's voice; tessitura 200-540 Hz sits in a mezzo zone.
- Ring-mod ghost carrier at 40 Hz is panned centre, dry — suggests the faint
  hum of copper lines.
- csdr ragtime stride quarter-note pulse, softer than track 01.
- Short plosive clicks (Praat Dirac impulses) stand in for plug jacks on the
  chorus downbeats.

## Pronunciation hints
- "switchboard" as `'s w I tS b O r d` — the `tS` is safe in us1.
- "number" as `'n V m b @` — keep the final schwa; no rhotic tail.
- Avoid "copper" clusters; sing `'k A p @` with a gentle stop.
- "secret" as `'s i k r @ t` — let the `k r` glide, don't over-articulate.

## Character
Sally is warm, competent, slightly bemused. No wink, no cynicism. We keep
her tender. The chorus is a small ritual phrase — the motif `m.hook` is the
four-note figure on "Sally, Sally, open the door".
