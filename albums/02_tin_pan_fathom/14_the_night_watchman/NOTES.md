# The Night Watchman — notes

12-bar TPA form in A major at 94 bpm. 3 x 12 = 36 bars.

## Chord implication
Each 12-bar chorus: I-IV-I-I | IV-IV-I-I | ii-V-I-V7. `m.tin_pan_turnaround`
anchors bars 9-12.

## Production shape
- Vox (us3), tessitura 170-480 Hz — warm and low.
- Ring-mod ghost at 40 Hz is unmodulated, a constant soft drone.
- csdr stride: a single heavy bass pulse on beat 1 of each measure only,
  leaving the rest of the bar to vocal and ghost — the watchman's footsteps.
- Klatt texture: a faint whistle on the word "whistles", lightly.
- Praat-shaped lantern click (broadband transient, 50 ms) once per 12-bar
  at bar 6 — sounds like a shutter lamp being lifted.

## Pronunciation hints
- "watchman" as `'w A tS m @ n`.
- "lantern" as `'l & n t @ n`.
- "whistles" as `'w I s @ l z`.
- "Sousa" as `'s u s @` — proper noun but the phonemes are safe.
- "patient" as `'p eI S @ n t` — keep the `St` light.
- Avoid "alley"'s `ll`-stall; sing `'& l i` one syllable-bridge.

## Character
We walk with him. The watchman is a civic presence, not a warning.
`m.hook` is the refrain "The evening is a..." setup that invites a new
noun each verse.
