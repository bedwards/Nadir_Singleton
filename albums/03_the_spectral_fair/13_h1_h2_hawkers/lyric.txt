Hawker on the left and a hawker on the right
A breathy little offer and a pressed a little tight
First against the second is a measure of a moan
A H one and a H two and a hum and a bone

Breathy on a penny, pressed a little steel
A difference of a decibel a hawker can reveal
We listen to the calling and we follow every sigh
A first above the second and a second lifted high

Hawker on the corner holds a value of a moan
A difference of a decibel is a currency of tone
We buy a little breathy, and we buy a little hard
And tuck a little ratio in a pocket for regard
