Lights a little lower on the midway and the lane
A lantern for a loudness and a lantern for a plane
A lantern for a jitter and a lantern for a shine
A lantern for a harmony, a lantern for a line

One by one the bulbs go gently out along the row
A mellow little meter as the midway lets it go
The hawkers and the horses and the banners and the bell
All of them are sleeping and the night is doing well

Lights a little lower and a little little tune
A lantern for a memory beneath a little moon
We tuck away the waltz and we count a little more
And carry every lantern to the quiet of the door
