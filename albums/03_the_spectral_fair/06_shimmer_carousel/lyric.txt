Up and down and up and down the painted horses go
A decibel a little more, a decibel a little low
We ride a little higher when the organ pipes a call
A shimmer on a saddle on a little horse of brass

Round and round and round, the carousel remembers
Every gentle up, every gentle down, every gentle ember
We measure every rising, we measure every fall
And write it in a ledger that the organ carries all

Up and down and up and down, the painted horses go
A shimmer on a saddle and a little of a glow
We ride until the music lets a little of it slow
And step away, and wave away, and let the wagon go
