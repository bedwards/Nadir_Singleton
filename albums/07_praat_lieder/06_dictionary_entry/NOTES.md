# 06 Dictionary Entry — notes

## Why this slot
The first of the two major-key tracks on the album. We need a pocket of
warmth between 05's held sodium note and 07's ritual return to the
horizon-salts motif. A dictionary entry is the only object in the cycle
that is also language about language.

## Form and motif deployment
Through-composed, 16 bars in C major at 64 bpm, 4/4. Slightly livelier
tempo. `m.whisper_psola` still covers the whole track — the key being
major does not mean the treatment changes; PSOLA de-voicing continues to
mute drama. The track smiles by harmony, not by voice.

## Praat treatment
Leans on **`psola_retarget.praat`** from `nadir-praat-scripts`. Pitch
extraction autocorrelation (`ac`), floor 75 Hz, ceiling 1100 Hz (slightly
raised for the brighter key), very-accurate on. Target median 230 Hz.
Range factor 0.82 — the loosest compression on the album, because a
smile needs a little melodic room. Formant shift ratio 1.06 — a
perceptible upward shift that brightens without cartooning.

## csdr graph shape
`band_limit` preset at -24 dB, 220–2800 Hz. Slightly wider band than the
minor-key tracks, to give the major tonality headroom. Bed gated off
the last 2 bars (closing the book). No reverb.

## G2P / pronunciation hints
- "noun" as /n aU n/ — a single stressed syllable, quite short.
- "origin" as /'O r @ dZ I n/ — four syllables, stress first; the /dZ/
  must be voiced (like "edge").
- "latin" as /'l & t n=/ — syllabic /n=/ on the second syllable.
- "mouth" as a verb /m aU D/ — voiced final (like the verb "smooth"),
  not /m aU T/ like the noun.
- "definition" as /d E f @ 'n I S @ n/ — four syllables, stress on 'n I S'.

## openSMILE gate
Voicing fraction in [0.38, 0.58] — the major key and brighter formants
raise it. Pitch-error RMS 2 cents ceiling. Loudness -17 LUFS integrated.
