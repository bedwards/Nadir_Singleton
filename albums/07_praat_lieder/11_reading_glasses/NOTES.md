# 11 Reading Glasses — notes

## Why this slot
The second and last major-key Lied. Track 06 Dictionary Entry was major
and smiled; this track is major and sighs gently. We pair these two as
the two moments where the cycle's gravity lifts slightly — but here the
subject is forgetfulness, so the major key is affectionate rather than
playful.

## Form and motif deployment
Through-composed, 16 bars in F major at 66 bpm, 4/4. Slightly brighter
tempo than the minor-key neighbours. `m.whisper_psola` continues; the
major key does not exempt a track from PSOLA de-voicing. The melodic
contour has more room to move (range factor 0.80) than the minor tracks,
but the voicing amplitude is still reduced.

## Praat treatment
Leans on **`psola_retarget.praat`** from `nadir-praat-scripts`. Pitch
extraction autocorrelation (`ac`), floor 75 Hz, ceiling 1100 Hz,
very-accurate on. Target median 235 Hz. Range factor 0.80. Formant shift
ratio 1.08 — the highest upward shift on the album, matching the slight
brightness of F major. We want a voice just above our natural register,
which fits the affectionate forgetful tone.

## csdr graph shape
`band_limit` preset at -24 dB, wide band 220–2800 Hz. Bed gated off for
the very last bar (glasses will be picked up tomorrow — the thought
trails into silence). No reverb.
