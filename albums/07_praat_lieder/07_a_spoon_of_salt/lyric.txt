a spoon
of salt
in the palm
we look at it
we do not take it yet
the grains are small
the spoon is old
the silver has lost its shine
the way a coin does
after it has been counted many times
we remember the first salt
the small salt
on the tongue of the morning
we do not say those words now
we only hold the spoon
the spoon holds us back
and the morning begins again
a little to the left
